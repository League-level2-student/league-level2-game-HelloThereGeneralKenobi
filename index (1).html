Super Frogger

by: Emanuele Rimini

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game because I wanted to make a bullet hell game that could be played forever and had increasing difficulty. I also wanted to make it somewhat resemble Rush Hour the board game and the iconic video game classic Frogger.

2. What I Learned

I learned that my game was unclear on what I had to do and I changed it to be less text heavy and more show instead of tell.

3. Architecture of My Game

I made the game possible to go on forever which constantly increasing difficulty so it could have lots of replay-ability and not get boring. The score interacts with the difficulty because increasing your score increases how difficult the game is.

4. Technical Implementation

I liked how the cars came from all sides at random values and it made it seem complicated because there were so many things on your screen but in reality it was very simple.

5. Most Challenging Part

Hardest part was getting all the cars to come from all 4 sides at once while looking good and smooth.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I could optimize the code and I would improve the game by adding power-ups or power-downs.
